## 2. Target Audience

**Primary segment — The Young Damascus Explorer (18–30)**

University students, young professionals, and creatives living in or regularly visiting Damascus. This group is hungry
for new experiences and community after years of disruption. They are highly active on Instagram and TikTok, share
moments in real time, and discover places through friends and influencers rather than traditional advertising. They care
about authenticity, local pride, and finding events that match their vibe — concerts, art nights, tech meetups, and
rooftop gatherings.

Pain points: No single place to find out what's actually happening in the city tonight. Word-of-mouth is fragmented
across WhatsApp groups, Instagram stories, and Facebook pages. Missing events they would have loved.

Channels they use: Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp groups, university networks.

Buying stage: Unaware → Awareness (this is a launch, so the goal is discovery and first download).

**Secondary segment — Venue Owners & Event Organizers**

Café owners, cultural space managers, independent event promoters in Damascus who want better reach for their events and
a loyalty tool that keeps customers coming back. They are the supply side of the platform and must be onboarded before
users arrive.

Pain points: Limited digital tools for local promotion. No way to reach people walking nearby. No loyalty program
without expensive hardware.

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Instagram (Primary — Organic + Paid)

The dominant visual platform for 18–35 Damascenes. Instagram Reels showing the map UI, event pins dropping, and real
check-in moments will resonate immediately. Run geo-targeted Instagram ads to Damascus with a $30–50/day budget for the
launch week.

Format: Reels (15–30 sec), Stories with swipe-up to App Store/Play Store, carousel posts for feature highlights. Effort:
High. Budget allocation: ~$800 of paid.

### TikTok (Primary — Organic)

Fast-growing in Syria, especially among under-25s. Raw, authentic content performs — someone opening the app, seeing
events near them for the first time, reacting. Partner with 2–3 Damascus-based TikTok creators for "what's on tonight?"
content. No paid spend needed if creator content lands organically.

Format: 15–45 sec POV-style videos, creator partnerships. Effort: Medium. Budget: ~$600 in creator fees.

### WhatsApp (Secondary — Community seeding)

Damascus runs on WhatsApp groups — university groups, neighborhood groups, event circles. Seed early access links and
invite codes into 10–15 targeted groups through trusted community members. This is your highest-conversion channel
because it's peer-to-peer.

Format: Short message + link + screenshot of the map. Effort: Low. Budget: $0.

### Local Influencers & Content Creators (Secondary — Earned)

Identify 5–8 Damascus-based lifestyle, food, and events creators on Instagram/TikTok with 5k–50k followers. Offer free
venue partner access, early app access, and a small fee ($50–$150 per creator). Ask them to authentically cover an event
using the app.

Format: Story takeovers, Reels using the app, map screenshots. Effort: Medium. Budget: ~$600 total.

### Venue Partner Network (Owned — Critical)

Before the app goes public, personally onboard 30+ Damascus venues. These partners become distribution channels: they
tell their customers about the app, display QR codes, and incentivize check-ins with their own loyalty rewards. Venue
partners are the supply-side launch requirement.

Format: In-person visits, WhatsApp pitch, printed QR code materials. Effort: High. Budget: ~$300 for printed materials.

### University Campuses (Earned — Grassroots)

Damascus University, Syrian Private University, and Arab International University have large, digitally active student
populations. Partner with student unions or campus clubs to run EventMap-sponsored events. First on-campus event = first
batch of Vibe Stories.

Format: Campus event, poster, campus group WhatsApp. Effort: Medium. Budget: ~$500 for sponsored event.

### App Store Optimization / Landing Page (Owned)

Before the app launches, build a simple Arabic/English landing page at the domain with email/phone waitlist capture.
Optimize App Store listing with Arabic keywords, Damascus screenshots, and a strong description. Effort: Medium.
Budget: $0 (internal).

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: Low venue supply at launch.**
If fewer than 15 venues are verified by launch day, the map looks empty and first-time users will churn immediately.
*Mitigation:* Begin venue outreach 3 weeks before launch (Week 1). Set a hard gate — do not launch publicly until 20+
venues are live on the map. Offer venue owners a "Founding Partner" badge as an incentive.

**Risk 2: Connectivity and device fragmentation.**
Syria has real infrastructure variability. A heavy map app may perform poorly on older Android devices or slower
connections.
*Mitigation:* Test the Flutter app on mid-range Android devices (the dominant segment) and on 3G connections before
launch. Optimize tile loading. Add offline messaging if map fails to load.

**Risk 3: Creator content underperforms.**
If creator Reels don't hit organic reach, the pre-launch momentum stalls.
*Mitigation:* Brief creators with specific formats known to perform (POV discovery, reaction content) rather than
leaving it open. Diversify across 5+ creators so performance isn't dependent on one. Keep $300 of the contingency budget
ready to boost the best-performing creator post as a paid collab.
